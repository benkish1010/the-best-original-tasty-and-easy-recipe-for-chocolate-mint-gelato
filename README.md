## üç® What Makes It "Gelato" vs "Ice Cream"?

Understanding the difference between gelato and ice cream is crucial for achieving authentic results. These aren't just different names for the same dessert - they're fundamentally different products with distinct textures, flavors, and serving temperatures.

**The core differences:**

| Feature | Gelato (This Recipe) | American Ice Cream |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Milk:Cream Ratio** | Higher milk (3:1) - 600g milk to 200g cream | Higher cream (1:1 or more) |
| **Fat Content** | 6-9% total fat | 14-25% total fat |
| **Overrun (Air)** | 20-35% air incorporation | 50-100% air incorporation |
| **Serving Temp** | -12¬∞C to -14¬∞C (10-14¬∞F) - slightly warmer | -18¬∞C to -23¬∞C (0-10¬∞F) - rock hard |
| **Texture** | Denser, silkier, more intense | Lighter, fluffier, airier |
| **Churning Speed** | Slower churning = less air | Faster churning = more air |
| **Flavor Intensity** | More intense (less fat coating tongue) | More muted (fat coats taste buds) |

**Why these differences matter:**

The lower fat content in gelato means flavors hit your taste buds more directly without being muted by butterfat. The denser texture (less air) creates a richer mouthfeel despite having less actual fat. Serving gelato slightly warmer than ice cream allows the flavors to be more pronounced and the texture to be creamier. This recipe is specifically designed to achieve these authentic gelato characteristics using a standard home ice cream maker.

## üìä Recipe Overview

### Yield
- **About 1 quart / 1 liter** (depends on machine and overrun)
- Serves: 8-10 people

### Time Requirements
- **Prep time:** 30 minutes
- **Infusion:** 10-15 minutes
- **Cooking:** 15-20 minutes
- **Chilling:** 4-24 hours (longer is better)
- **Churning:** 15-30 minutes
- **Freezing:** 2-4 hours
- **Total:** 7-27 hours (mostly passive)

### Ingredients (Metric Recommended)

#### Base
- **600 g** whole milk (3.25% fat)
- **200 g** heavy cream (35% fat or higher)
- **150 g** granulated sugar
- **4** large egg yolks (about 75-85 g)
- **25-35 g** fresh mint leaves (stems removed, washed and dried)
- **1/4 tsp** fine salt

#### Chocolate Components
- **35-45 g** unsweetened cocoa powder (Dutch-process recommended)
- **80-120 g** dark chocolate (60-70% cacao), finely chopped
- **50 g** mini chocolate chips (optional, for stracciatella effect)

### Equipment Needed
- Heavy-bottom saucepan
- Whisk
- Fine-mesh strainer
- Instant-read thermometer (crucial!)
- Ice cream maker
- Storage container
- Large bowl for ice bath

## üìñ Detailed Instructions

**Goal:** Extract fresh mint flavor without bitterness

1. Combine **milk (600g)** + **cream (200g)** + **mint leaves (25-35g)** in a heavy-bottom pot
2. Heat on medium until mixture is **steaming** but NOT boiling (~80¬∞C / 176¬∞F)
3. Remove from heat, cover, and steep for **10 minutes**
4. Taste test: Should have clear mint flavor. If too mild, steep 5 more minutes
5. Strain out mint leaves using fine-mesh strainer

### Step 2: Add Cocoa + Melt Chocolate (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Fully dissolve cocoa and chocolate for smooth texture

1. While mixture is still warm, whisk in:
   - **Sugar (150g)**
   - **Cocoa powder (35-45g)**
   - **Salt (1/4 tsp)**
2. Whisk thoroughly until no cocoa lumps remain
3. Add **chopped dark chocolate (80-120g)**

   - If needed, return to very low heat
   - Don't overheat or chocolate can seize

**Temperature:** 60-70¬∞C (140-158¬∞F)  
**Time:** 5 minutes

### Step 3: Temper Eggs + Cook Custard (8-12 minutes)

**Goal:** Create silky custard base without scrambling eggs

1. In a separate bowl, whisk **egg yolks (4)** until smooth
2. **Temper the eggs** (crucial step!):
   - Ladle 1/4 cup hot chocolate mixture into eggs while whisking constantly
   - Add another 1/4 cup, whisking constantly
   - Add one more 1/4 cup
   - This gradually raises egg temperature
3. Pour tempered egg mixture back into pot
4. Cook on **medium-low heat**, stirring constantly with wooden spoon or silicone spatula
5. Cook until mixture reaches **82-84¬∞C (180-183¬∞F)**
   - Should coat the back of a spoon
   - Run your finger through the coating - line should remain
   - ‚ö†Ô∏è Do NOT exceed 85¬∞C or eggs will scramble!
6. Remove from heat immediately when temperature is reached

**Target Temperature:** 82-84¬∞C (180-183¬∞F)  
**Time:** 8-12 minutes  
**Why:** This temperature kills bacteria while creating perfect custard texture

### Step 4: Chill Hard (4-24 hours)

**Goal:** Fully chill base and develop flavor

1. Optional but recommended: Strain through fine-mesh strainer for ultra-smooth texture
2. **Quick chill:**
   - Prepare ice bath (large bowl with ice + water)
   - Place pot in ice bath
   - Stir occasionally for 30 minutes until cool
3. Transfer to storage container and cover surface with plastic wrap (prevents skin)
4. Refrigerate for **minimum 4 hours**, ideally **overnight (12-24 hours)**
   - This allows flavors to develop
   - Cold base churns better
   - Fat crystallizes properly

**Temperature:** Below 4¬∞C (39¬∞F)  
**Time:** 4-24 hours (longer is better)

### Step 5: Churn (15-30 minutes) + Add Chips

**Goal:** Incorporate minimal air for dense gelato texture

1. Pre-freeze ice cream maker bowl according to manufacturer instructions (usually 12-24 hours)
2. Pour chilled base into ice cream maker
3. Churn according to machine instructions
   - Usually **15-30 minutes**
   - Should reach soft-serve consistency
   - Should pull away from sides slightly
4. **Optional:** In last 1-2 minutes, add **mini chocolate chips (50g)** for stracciatella effect
5. Gelato will be soft but should hold its shape

**Time:** 15-30 minutes (machine dependent)  
**Texture:** Soft-serve consistency

### Step 6: Freeze + Serve (2-4 hours)

**Goal:** Firm up gelato to scoopable consistency

1. Transfer gelato to freezer-safe container
2. Press plastic wrap directly on surface (prevents ice crystals)
3. Freeze for **2-4 hours** until firm but scoopable
4. **Serving tip:** Let gelato sit at room temperature for **5-10 minutes** before scooping
   - This brings it to ideal gelato serving temperature
   - Flavors are more pronounced when not rock-hard
   - Texture becomes silkier

**Storage:** Up to 2 weeks in freezer  
**Serving Temperature:** -12¬∞C to -14¬∞C (10-14¬∞F)

## üí° Pro Tips

### Mint Intensity
- **Too mild?** Next batch use 40g mint or steep 15 minutes
- **Too strong?** Use 25g mint or steep only 8-10 minutes
- **Pro trick:** Tear mint leaves before infusing to release more oils
- **Never boil mint** - it becomes bitter and grassy

### Chocolate Options
- **Dutch-process cocoa:** Less acidic, darker color (recommended)
- **Natural cocoa:** More acidic, brighter flavor
- **Dark chocolate:** 60-70% is sweet spot (not too bitter, not too sweet)
- **For intense chocolate:** Use 45g cocoa + 120g chocolate

### Texture Optimization
- **Denser gelato:** Churn until thick, pack tightly (minimize air)
- **Silkier gelato:** Don't rush the chilling phase (12-24 hours is ideal)
- **Softer gelato:** Add 10-20g more sugar (sugar prevents hardness)
- **Prevent ice crystals:** Press plastic wrap directly on surface during storage

### Equipment Tips
- **No thermometer?** Use the spoon coating test, but thermometer is highly recommended
- **No ice cream maker?** Place in freezer and stir vigorously every 30 minutes for 3-4 hours (labor intensive but works)
- **Machine too small?** Make half batch

## üîß Common Issues & Solutions

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Grainy texture** | Eggs scrambled | Keep temperature at 82-84¬∞C, stir constantly |
| **Icy texture** | Not enough fat/sugar | Add 10g more sugar or 50g more cream next time |
| **Too hard to scoop** | Too much water | Increase sugar by 10-20g |
| **Mint too weak** | Not enough steeping | Use 40g mint or steep 15 minutes |
| **Mint too grassy** | Over-extracted | Steep less time, don't crush leaves |
| **Cocoa lumps** | Added to cold liquid | Add cocoa to warm liquid and whisk thoroughly |
| **Chocolate seized** | Water contamination | Keep all utensils dry when melting chocolate |
